Chicken Congee

A recipe for chicken congee that can be eaten when you are either feeling sick or want a comfort soup. A tasty porridge for everyone of any age to enjoy.

Ingredients

  • 3 cups of water
  • 3 chicken thighs
  • 2 stalks green onion
  • 1 medium or large onion
  • 1/2 cup uncooked rice
  • Salt
  • Pepper
  • Msg
  • Fish sauce

Directions

  1. Bring the water with the chicken thighs to a boil on medium high to high heat. Season broth with 2 tsp of salt, 2 tsp of fish sauce and 1 tsp MSG. Boil until chicken thighs are cook (about 25-30 minutes). Make sure to skim off impurities from top of broth.
  2. Chop green onions and set aside for the end to garish. Chop half onion and dice one half and set aside for garish. Boil other half of onion in broth.
  3. Once chicken thighs are cooked, take out and place in a bowl to cool down. Once cooled down enough either shred or chop the chicken and place back into broth.
  4. While waiting for chicken to cool, rinse the rice until water is clear. Once rinsed, place into broth and cook on high heat until rice is soft.
  5. Congee is done whenever the rice is soft or at the consistency you prefer. Ladel into bowl and season with more salt, fish sauce and pepper to preferred taste. Garish with green onions and diced onion and enjoy!
